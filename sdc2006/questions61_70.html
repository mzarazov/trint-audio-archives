Q66. This may make you say "uncle". 

No doubt many aue-ers mourned the death of a certain person
during the year since the last SDC--we say "no doubt" because
his death wasn't mentioned here. He's connected, in a way that
*has* been mentioned in the group, with the following places:
Layton, Florida; Cove, Oregon; Bladworth, Saskatchewan;
Felchville, Vermont. Add another place to the list, with
supporting evidence.

H. He died in Wales.

A. Any place that's 24 hours from Tulsa according to MapQuest or
one of its competitors.

E. The person is Gene Pitney. "Uncle"
because of the Rolling Stones song "Now I've Got a Witness (Like
Uncle Phil and Uncle Gene)", which refers to Phil Spector and
Pitney.

Q67. Or Kylie? 

What is the next word in this series? Aurochs, building, camel,
__. Please explain.

H. The next word could be "door" or "dace". Now what's the
word after that? Please explain.
(The answer is then some word starting with "e" and related to
praying, calling, or jubilation--maybe "evensong", "entreaty",
or "exultation".)

A. Door, or any fish name beginning with "d", such as dace.

E. The letter "a" comes from a Semitic pictogram for an
ox head, "b" for a house, "c" for a camel (or a throwing stick),
"d" for either a door or a fish.
Title: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boomerang#Related_terms


Jerry/ Paul
Q68. Long live, or live long 

Solve this cryptic crossword clue:
Do we hear merrymaking burn the French boundary? (7, 2)

A. Charles IX.

E. Prosper Merimee wrote about Charles IX, so the
latter is a Merimee king. Burn = char, the (French) = le,
boundary = six. "Long live" for the king, "live long" for
Prosper.
